# Design direction

## Selected direction

The portfolio uses a Swiss-modernist technical editorial system: corporate credibility with enough developer personality to feel distinctive. A twelve-column grid, oversized typography, thin rules, strong alignment, and selective asymmetry guide recruiters from identity to work, experience, credentials, and contact.

## Visual system

- **Layout:** editorial grid, 1240px maximum content width, alternating deep navy and warm-white sections.
- **Typography:** Lexend for display and headings; Source Sans 3 for body and interface text.
- **Colour:** deep navy, paper white, ink, muted grey, and action blue with AA-conscious contrast.
- **Shape and texture:** mostly rectangular compositions, thin borders, restrained shadows, grid lines, and index labels.
- **Assets:** optimized WebP portrait and application screenshots with honest fallbacks where an image fails.

## Conversion and credibility

The hero quickly establishes name, role, location, education, focus, availability, GitHub, and contact. Featured projects precede the skills and experience details so recruiters see evidence early. Current employment and responsibilities are visible without inflated metrics. Live-demo and resume actions render only when their targets are verified.

## Accessibility and motion

- Semantic landmarks, logical headings, a skip link, visible focus rings, and keyboard-operable navigation.
- Content remains visible without animation; motion enhances rather than gates information.
- Reduced-motion mode disables transforms, parallax, and carousel autoplay while preserving manual controls.
- Controls meet a 44px minimum target and layouts reflow at mobile widths and a 200%-zoom equivalent.

Avoid fake terminals, icon clouds, skill ratings, scroll hijacking, excessive pills, and decorative animation without purpose.
